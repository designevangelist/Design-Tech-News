African Startup Ecosystem Raises $2.4B in Q1 2025 — A 40% YoY Surge

Fintech continues to lead, but edtech and health-tech are gaining ground fast across East and West Africa.

Africa’s startup ecosystem recorded its strongest Q1 performance yet, with $2.4 billion raised across 320 deals — a 40% increase compared to Q1 2024.

Key Highlights

Nigeria, Kenya, Egypt, and South Africa continue to dominate deal flow, accounting for 78% of total funding. However, francophone Africa is emerging fast, with Senegal and Côte d’Ivoire seeing record activity.

Sector Breakdown

Fintech remains the dominant sector at 42% of total funding. Edtech surged 120% year-over-year as demand for skills training grows across the continent. Health-tech attracted significant investor interest following several high-profile exits.

What This Means

Investors are increasingly confident in African founders building for local problems with global potential. The data signals a maturing ecosystem with stronger fundamentals than ever before.
